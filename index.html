Times University Ranking System 2016

How does it work?

Introduction

The results of the ranking of universities are calculated every year by Times Higher Education Ranking. The results are a combination of few categories. The categories consist of Teaching, International Outlook, Research, Citations, and Industry Income. The "Teaching", "Research", and "Citations" holds 30% weight each in the overall score. Then, "International Outlook" holds 7.5% weight in the overall score. Lastly, "Industry Income" holds 2.5% weight in the overall score. Times Higher Education rankings is often considered one of the most widely observed university rankings.

The purpose behind the ranking system defined by Times Higher Education is to allow the young ones to further understand what they are getting into when they are choosing the desired university. Moreover, the ranking system gives a clear difference between different universities in the world.

Furthermore, it can be observed that the category citations has outlier which means abnormalities recorded in the data from Times Higher Education.
